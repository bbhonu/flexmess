STAY WOKE: CELIAC DISEASE

A Dangerous Trend

Those who have been diagnosed know how much it is trivialized by restaurants and the media due to the spike in the trendiness of a gluten-free diet.

This has lead to a reduction in the safety of eating out because it isn’t taken as seriously. But celiac disease is not a joke for the 1% of the population that it does affect.

What is Celiac Disease?

It’s not an allergy or an intolerance.

Celiac is a hereditary autoimmune disease. When gluten is consumed by someone with Celiac, their immune system attacks the part of the body where nutrients are absorbed.

This inflammatory response causes severe GI pain and can lead to and various issues like malnourishment, dermatitis herpetiformis, lymphoma, anemia, depression, liver disease, and osteopenia (weak bones).

What is gluten?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat (including wheatberries, durum, emmer, semolina, spelt, farina, farro, and graham), barley, and rye.

Gluten is Good

(for most people)

So are those who say that they’re “gluten intolerant” faking it? Not necessarily.

They probably fall into one of several groups:

  • - Celiac hasn’t fully developed yet so they can’t be properly diagnosed.
  • - They have a wheat allergy (there is no such thing as a gluten allergy.)
  • - They are sensitive to FODMAPs, a type of carbohydrate that includes wheat.
